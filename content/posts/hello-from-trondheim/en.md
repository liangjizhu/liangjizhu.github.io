I grew up in Madrid. For the last few months I have been living in Trondheim, a small Norwegian university town that gets dark in December and stays bright until midnight in June. This is where I finally gave in and started writing.

## Why a blog

I already have a CV. The CV is honest but compressed: a couple of lines per job and project, enough to pass a screen. It does not show how I think, where I got stuck, or what I learned the hard way.

A blog fits the gaps. I want a place to:

- explain the problems I am working on, in my own words;
- keep notes that future-me can actually search;
- practice writing the same ideas in **English**, **Spanish** and **Chinese**.

## What I expect to write about

- **Robotics simulation.** I work with [Vortex NTNU](https://www.vortexntnu.no/) on an underwater-drone project. ROS2, C++, Stonefish, and the constant surprise of sensors that do not behave like their datasheet.
- **Systems and algorithms.** Short notes from coursework, from `gdb` sessions, from the moments when a data structure finally clicks.
- **Living in three languages.** Notes on switching between Chinese, Spanish and English — what changes when the language changes.

## A small note on format

Posts are written as plain Markdown. If a post only exists in one language, I mark it clearly — I would rather publish one careful translation than three rushed ones.

> If you see a typo, it is not a metaphor. Send me an email.

Welcome. More soon.
